About

Hello traveler! I'm Visweswaran Baskaran, a passionate explorer and builder in the realm of robotics. Currently, I'm pursuing my Master's in Robotics at the GRASP Lab at the University of Pennsylvania, focusing on Computer Vision, Deep Learning, and Robot Perception.

Previously, I earned my Bachelor's in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from the National Institute of Technology, Tiruchirappalli. During the summer of 2022, I worked on legged robots at the Dynamic Robotics and Control Lab at USC Viterbi under the guidance of Prof. Quan Nguyen through the IUSSTF-Viterbi Scholarship Program. Prior to that, I conducted research on robust teleoperated systems at the Robotics Lab in IIT Madras under the supervision of Prof. Asokan Thondiyath.

In essence, I'm enthusiastic about all things robotics (or movies) and am always open to a chat!

Professional Interest

I'm passionate about exploring and building foundational models for robots to see, think and act through perception, learning and control respectively. In particular, I'm keen on exploring the seamless intersection of these three domains to create robust algorithms. I want to use the prior knowledge from classical perception and control approaches to optimize robot learning and the vice versa, that is to use state-of-the art deep learning methods to learn a better model for classical algorithms.
